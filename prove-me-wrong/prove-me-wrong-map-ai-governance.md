##  The Challenge Questions — Upgraded

### 1. Decision Ownership `[OAL Gate]`

When an AI-generated insight is produced inside a MAP-facilitated environment:

- Who **owns** the decision? Named individual — not "the team," not "leadership"
- Is that ownership **explicit** (documented, assigned) or **implied** (assumed, distributed)?
- What happens when the named owner is **unavailable or changes**?
- Does the MAP system enforce **single-threaded ownership** — one execution owner, one trigger authority, one verification owner?

> *OAL (Ownership Assignment Logic) fires on every action. Vague ownership — "team / leadership / stakeholders" — is a blocking condition. The question is whether MAP's system enforces this or assumes it.*

---

### 2. Trigger Mechanism `[DBL Gate — T2 Data / T3 Governance]`

What event **triggers action** from an AI-generated signal?

- Meeting cadence? *(time-based — not condition-based)*
- Threshold breach? *(condition-based — stronger)*
- Manual interpretation by consultant? *(human-dependent — slowest)*
- Automated routing to named owner? *(system-executed — fastest)*

> *The gap between "insight exists" and "action is triggered" is where most AI ROI disappears. The MIT 95% zero ROI finding lives here. The trigger mechanism is the binding constraint.*

---

### 3. Verification Loop `[APVL — Post-Deployment Validation]`

How is execution **confirmed** after action is triggered?

- Self-reported by client? *(lowest reliability)*
- System-tracked against a defined metric? *(medium reliability)*
- Audited against baseline at Day 30 / Day 90? *(highest reliability)*

> *APVL (AIS Post-Deployment Validation Loop) fires at Day 30 and Day 90. Insights that pass the action gate but lose their named owner or trigger condition by Day 30 re-enter INSIGHT THEATER state. Does MAP's system have an equivalent?*

---

### 4. Accountability Velocity `[DLS — Decision Latency Score]`

What is the **measured time** between:

```
AI Insight generated
      ↓
Ownership assigned (named individual)
      ↓
Trigger condition confirmed
      ↓
Action executed
      ↓
Outcome verified against baseline
```

> *Decision Latency Score (DLS) measures this gap. MAP's current DLS is estimated at 55/100 — consultant-led model creates structural latency. The question is whether MAP has a mechanism to compress this cycle, or whether the meeting cadence is the ceiling.*

---

### 5. Compliance Readiness `[NEW — CO AI Act June 2026]`

When a MAP client deploys AI in a consequential decision — hiring, credit, healthcare triage, performance management:

- Does MAP have a framework for confirming that deployment is **CO AI Act compliant**?
- Does MAP's accountability system include a **regulatory exposure audit** as a standard deliverable?
- Who owns the compliance posture on the MAP side — and what is their AI governance credential?

> *The CO AI Act enforcement window opens June 2026. MAP's clients in Colorado, California, and regulated industries are in the exposure zone. This is not a future risk. It is a 60-day forcing function.*
